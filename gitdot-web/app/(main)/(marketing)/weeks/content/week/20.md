There is something we want to build.

We didn’t know what that was when we started off as an “indie hacker” duo and we lost sight of it when we pivoted into “doing something big.” 

I want to be clear on what that is. \
I want there to be no misgivings about what it is we’re doing and what it is we’re _not_ doing either.

So here it is.

&nbsp;

## 1. Our goal is to last
We like what we’re doing and intend on doing it for a very long time.
That doesn’t mean we won’t be ambitious, we’re the type to and we know, but it does mean that we do not obsess over growth at all costs.

## 2. Our brand is quality
We hate the ethos of doing things as quickly as possible as shitty as possible.
We take pride in what we do: from the lines of code we write to every detail we design.
We build software _well_ — not because it is the optimal thing to do, but because it is the _right_ thing to do.

## 3. Our mission is to contest the norm
It is to be true to who we are, knowing that may make us unpopular or even unprofitable.
It is work with integrity: to build for the now rather than guess at the new, to listen to our customers instead of our own egos, and to be honest in each and every thing we do.


&nbsp;

It is our hope to posit an alternative. \
We say this with humility, knowing that the road ahead will be long and untrodden.

But we are grateful for the opportunity to walk it.

Thank you, \
—baepaul.
